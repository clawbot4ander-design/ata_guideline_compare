## How to Read This Table

### Column Definitions

| Column | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **#** | Row number for reference |
| **Domain** | Clinical topic area |
| **2015 Recommendation** | Exact recommendation number, key wording, and strength/evidence grading from the 2015 ATA guideline (ACP system: Strong/Weak, High/Moderate/Low quality) |
| **2025 Recommendation** | Exact recommendation number, key wording, and strength/evidence grading from the 2025 ATA guideline (modified GRADE: Strong/Conditional, High/Moderate/Low/Very Low certainty; GPS = Good Practice Statement) |
| **Change Type** | Categorization of the nature of change (see categories below) |
| **WHY It Changed (Rationale)** | The evidence, reasoning, or philosophy shift that drove the change, extracted from the guideline discussion text. Layer 2 of the three-layer analysis. |
| **Evidence Driver** | Category tag for the type of evidence or rationale (see categories below) |
| **Clinical Implication** | Who is affected, what changes in practice, and the magnitude of impact (HIGH / Moderate / Low). Layer 3 of the three-layer analysis. |
| **Confidence** | Confidence that the stated rationale is accurate: High = guideline explicitly states why; Moderate = strong inference from surrounding text; Low = inference with limited textual support |

### Change Type Categories

- **Unchanged** -- No meaningful difference between 2015 and 2025
- **Wording clarification** -- Different words, no clinical effect
- **Stronger recommendation** -- Increased recommendation strength, evidence quality, or directional clarity
- **Weaker recommendation** -- Decreased recommendation strength or narrowed certainty
- **Narrowed indication** -- Fewer patients qualify for the intervention
- **Broadened indication** -- More patients qualify for the intervention
- **Directional shift at lower certainty** -- The preferred direction changed, but the certainty of evidence decreased
- **Replaced framework** -- Entirely new system or structure replaces the prior one
- **Removed** -- Recommendation eliminated without replacement
- **Newly added** -- No corresponding 2015 recommendation
- **Elevated and expanded** -- Concept present in 2015 (possibly in a different section) but promoted to standalone recommendation with expanded detail
- **Reversed recommendation** -- Direction of recommendation changed (for to against, or vice versa)
- **Compounding effect** -- Cross-cutting change arising from the interaction of multiple individual changes

### Evidence Driver Categories

- **New evidence** -- Specific post-2015 studies, RCTs, or meta-analyses cited in the 2025 guideline
- **Reinterpretation** -- Same data, different conclusion
- **Outcome re-weighting** -- Different emphasis on endpoints (e.g., recurrence vs survival vs QoL)
- **De-escalation philosophy** -- Overtreatment concern, risk-adapted approach
- **Patient-centered** -- Shared decision-making, patient preference, morbidity avoidance
- **Implementation feedback** -- Real-world experience since 2015, feasibility concerns
- **Consensus shift** -- Expert opinion evolution without specific new data
- **Methodology change** -- Different evidence grading system, staging system, or classification
- **New technology** -- New diagnostic or therapeutic tools
- **Unclear** -- Guideline text does not explain; inference stated with confidence level

### Impact Scale

- **HIGH** -- Affects a large patient population OR changes a major clinical decision (surgery type, RAI use, medication requirement)
- **Moderate** -- Affects a defined subgroup OR refines an existing practice
- **Low** -- Minor wording update, rare entity, or confirms existing practice

## Scope Note

The 2015 ATA guideline covers both **thyroid nodules** (R1-R31) and **differentiated thyroid cancer** (R32-R101). The 2025 ATA guideline covers **differentiated thyroid cancer only**. This matrix compares DTC-relevant recommendations only. Where a 2015 nodule-section recommendation is relevant to a 2025 DTC recommendation (e.g., active surveillance), this is noted explicitly. Recommendations without a counterpart in the other guideline are marked as "Newly added" or "Out of 2025 scope."

## Methodology Notes

- **Grading system change:** 2015 uses the ACP system (Strong/Weak, High/Moderate/Low quality). 2025 uses modified GRADE (Strong/Conditional, High/Moderate/Low/Very Low certainty) plus Good Practice Statements (GPS). "Weak" (2015) approximately maps to "Conditional" (2025), but this is a methodology change, not necessarily a substantive strength change.
- **Staging system change:** 2015 references AJCC 7th edition; 2025 uses AJCC 8th edition. The age cutoff changed from 45 to 55 years, and microscopic ETE was removed from the T3 definition.
- **Risk stratification change:** 2015 uses a 3-tier ATA risk system (low, intermediate, high). 2025 uses a 4-tier ATA risk system (low, low-intermediate, intermediate-high, high).

## Narrative Summary: The 2025 Guideline in One Paragraph

The 2025 ATA DTC guideline represents the most significant paradigm shift in thyroid cancer management in a decade. Its dominant theme is **de-escalation for clearly low-risk disease** -- driven by the first Phase III RCT data (ESTIMABL2, HiLo), large meta-analyses, and population database reanalyses -- while **maintaining or intensifying treatment rigor for higher-risk disease and surgical quality standards** (IONM, LNR, LNY, targeted therapy). The compounding effect of lobectomy expansion + no RAI + no TSH suppression + surveillance endpoints transforms the low-risk patient journey from a years-long treatment cascade to a single surgery followed by time-limited monitoring. Simultaneously, precision medicine enters thyroid cancer management through the 4-tier risk stratification system and mutation-directed targeted therapy for RAIR disease. The guideline formalizes shared decision-making, introduces the concept of "complete remission" for DTC, and creates a formal non-surgical pathway (active surveillance) for microcarcinomas. Key areas of remaining uncertainty include the optimal management of intermediate-risk patients (conflicting RAI data), the appropriate TSH target in a world of "individualization," and the practical implementation of the 4-tier risk system across diverse practice settings.
