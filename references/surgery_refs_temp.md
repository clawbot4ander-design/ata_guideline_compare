## TOPIC 1: NCDB/SEER Lobectomy vs Total Thyroidectomy Survival Analyses

### 1A. Adam et al. 2014 -- NCDB 61,775 patients (KEY STUDY)

- **PMID**: 25203876
- **Citation**: Adam MA, Pura J, Gu L, Dinan MA, Tyler DS, Reed SD, Scheri R, Roman SA, Sosa JA. Extent of surgery for papillary thyroid cancer is not associated with survival: an analysis of 61,775 patients. *Ann Surg*. 2014 Oct;260(4):601-5; discussion 605-7.
- **DOI**: [10.1097/SLA.0000000000000925](https://doi.org/10.1097/SLA.0000000000000925)
- **Key finding**: Among 61,775 PTC patients (NCDB 1998-2006), after multivariable adjustment, overall survival was similar for total thyroidectomy vs lobectomy for tumors 1.0-4.0 cm (HR=0.96; 95% CI 0.84-1.09; P=0.54) and when stratified by size: 1.0-2.0 cm (HR=1.05; P=0.61) and 2.1-4.0 cm (HR=0.89; P=0.21).
- **Supports recommendation**: Lobectomy expansion to tumors up to 2 cm (and evidence base for 2-4 cm consideration)
- **Full text available**: Yes (PMC4532384)
- **Data source**: Abstract + Full text in PMC

### 1B. Adam et al. 2015 -- NCDB + SEER, age <45 years

- **PMID**: 25337927
- **Citation**: Adam MA, Pura J, Goffredo P, Dinan MA, Hyslop T, Reed SD, Scheri RP, Roman SA, Sosa JA. Impact of extent of surgery on survival for papillary thyroid cancer patients younger than 45 years. *J Clin Endocrinol Metab*. 2015 Jan;100(1):115-21.
- **DOI**: [10.1210/jc.2014-3039](https://doi.org/10.1210/jc.2014-3039)
- **Key finding**: In 29,522 NCDB + 13,510 SEER patients <45 years with stage I PTC 1.1-4.0 cm, OS was similar for total thyroidectomy vs lobectomy (NCDB: HR=1.45, P=0.19; SEER: HR=0.95, P=0.75), including stratified by tumor size 1.1-2.0 cm and 2.1-4.0 cm.
- **Supports recommendation**: Lobectomy expansion; no survival benefit for total thyroidectomy even in tumors up to 4 cm in younger patients
- **Full text available**: Yes (PMC5399499)
- **Data source**: Abstract + Full text in PMC

### 1C. Mendelsohn et al. 2010 -- SEER 22,724 patients

- **PMID**: 21079156
- **Citation**: Mendelsohn AH, Elashoff DA, Abemayor E, St John MA. Surgery for papillary thyroid carcinoma: is lobectomy enough? *Arch Otolaryngol Head Neck Surg*. 2010 Nov;136(11):1055-61.
- **DOI**: [10.1001/archoto.2010.181](https://doi.org/10.1001/archoto.2010.181)
- **Key finding**: Among 22,724 PTC patients (SEER 1988-2001), multivariate analysis controlling for tumor size revealed no survival difference between total thyroidectomy and lobectomy. Tumor size, extrathyroidal extent, nodal status, and age were significant predictors, but not extent of surgery.
- **Supports recommendation**: Early evidence supporting lobectomy adequacy; foundational study for de-escalation movement
- **Full text available**: No (journal article, not in PMC)
- **Data source**: Abstract

### 1D. Rajjoub et al. 2018 -- NCDB 33,816 patients, subtype analysis (COUNTERPOINT)

- **PMID**: 29426618
- **Citation**: Rajjoub SR, Yan H, Calcatera NA, Kuchta K, Wang CE, Lutfi W, Moo-Young TA, Winchester DJ, Prinz RA. Thyroid lobectomy is not sufficient for T2 papillary thyroid cancers. *Surgery*. 2018 May;163(5):1134-1143.
- **DOI**: [10.1016/j.surg.2017.12.026](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.surg.2017.12.026)
- **Key finding**: In 33,816 patients (NCDB 2004-2008), total thyroidectomy was associated with improved survival for 2.0-3.9 cm CONVENTIONAL PTC (P=0.03) but NOT for follicular-variant PTC. For 1.0-1.9 cm tumors, no difference regardless of subtype. Argues AGAINST lobectomy for T2 conventional PTC.
- **Supports recommendation**: Provides counterargument; supports caution with lobectomy for 2-4 cm conventional PTC without molecular subtyping
- **Full text available**: No (subscription required)
- **Data source**: Abstract

### 1E. Barney et al. 2010 -- SEER 23,605 patients

- **PMID**: 20687168
- **Citation**: Barney BM, Hitchcock YJ, Sharma P, Shrieve DC, Tward JD. Overall and cause-specific survival for patients undergoing lobectomy, near-total, or total thyroidectomy for differentiated thyroid cancer. *Head Neck*. 2011 May;33(5):645-9.
- **DOI**: [10.1002/hed.21504](https://doi.org/10.1002/hed.21504)
- **Key finding**: 10-year OS/CSS by surgery: total thyroidectomy 90.4%/96.8%, near-total 89.5%/96.6%, lobectomy 90.8%/98.6%. Near-total was inferior to total for OS (HR 1.21; P=.019) and CSS (HR 1.39; P=.019), but lobectomy was not inferior.
- **Supports recommendation**: Lobectomy yields comparable outcomes to total thyroidectomy; near-total may be inferior
- **Full text available**: No (subscription required)
- **Data source**: Abstract

## TOPIC 2: Prophylactic Central Neck Dissection (pCND) Meta-Analyses

### 2A. Chen et al. 2018 -- 18,376 patients meta-analysis (KEY STUDY)

- **PMID**: 29488066
- **Citation**: Chen L, Wu YH, Lee CH, Chen HA, Loh EW, Tam KW. Prophylactic central neck dissection for papillary thyroid carcinoma with clinically uninvolved central neck lymph nodes: a systematic review and meta-analysis. *World J Surg*. 2018 Sep;42(9):2846-2857.
- **DOI**: [10.1007/s00268-018-4547-4](https://doi.org/10.1007/s00268-018-4547-4)
- **Key finding**: 23 studies, 18,376 patients. pCND significantly reduced LRR (OR 0.65; 95% CI 0.48-0.88) BUT significantly increased transient RLN injury (OR 2.03), transient hypocalcemia (OR 2.23; 95% CI 1.84-2.70), and PERMANENT hypocalcemia (OR 2.22; 95% CI 1.58-3.13).
- **Supports recommendation**: 2025 recommendation AGAINST routine pCND -- the doubled hypocalcemia risk with questionable net benefit
- **Full text available**: No (subscription required for World J Surg)
- **Data source**: Abstract

### 2B. Zhao/Wang et al. 2017 -- 4,437 patients meta-analysis

- **PMID**: 27913945
- **Citation**: Zhao W, You L, Hou X, Chen S, Ren X, Chen G, Zhao Y. The effect of prophylactic central neck dissection on locoregional recurrence in papillary thyroid cancer after total thyroidectomy: a systematic review and meta-analysis. *Ann Surg Oncol*. 2017 Aug;24(8):2189-2198.
- **DOI**: [10.1245/s10434-016-5691-4](https://doi.org/10.1245/s10434-016-5691-4)
- **Key finding**: 17 studies, 4,437 patients. TT+pCND reduced LRR (RR=0.66; P=0.008), specifically in central neck compartment (RR=0.35; P=0.002), but increased temporary hypocalcemia (OR=2.37; P<0.00001), permanent hypocalcemia (OR=1.93; P=0.03), and overall morbidity (OR=2.56; P<0.00001).
- **Supports recommendation**: Evidence that pCND reduces recurrence but at cost of significantly increased complications
- **Full text available**: No (subscription required)
- **Data source**: Abstract

### 2C. Wang et al. 2013 -- Recurrence meta-analysis

- **PMID**: 23846784
- **Citation**: Wang TS, Cheung K, Farrokhyar F, Roman SA, Sosa JA. A meta-analysis of the effect of prophylactic central compartment neck dissection on locoregional recurrence rates in patients with papillary thyroid cancer. *Ann Surg Oncol*. 2013 Oct;20(11):3477-83.
- **DOI**: [10.1245/s10434-013-3125-0](https://doi.org/10.1245/s10434-013-3125-0)
- **Key finding**: 11 studies, 2,318 patients. Overall recurrence was 7.6% (TT alone) vs 4.7% (TT+pCCND); relative risk 0.59 (95% CI 0.33-1.07) -- a TREND toward lower recurrence but NOT statistically significant. NNT=31. No difference in permanent hypocalcemia or RLN injury rates.
- **Supports recommendation**: No definitive recurrence benefit from pCND; supports recommendation against routine pCND
- **Full text available**: No (subscription required)
- **Data source**: Abstract

### 2D. Lang et al. 2013 -- Short-term LRR meta-analysis

- **PMID**: 23402640
- **Citation**: Lang BH, Ng SH, Lau LL, Cowling BJ, Wong KP, Wan KY. A systematic review and meta-analysis of prophylactic central neck dissection on short-term locoregional recurrence in papillary thyroid carcinoma after total thyroidectomy. *Thyroid*. 2013 Sep;23(9):1087-98.
- **DOI**: [10.1089/thy.2012.0608](https://doi.org/10.1089/thy.2012.0608)
- **Key finding**: 14 studies, 3,331 patients. TT+pCND had 35% reduction in LRR risk (4.7% vs 8.6%; IRR=0.65; CI 0.48-0.86) but significantly more temporary hypocalcemia (26.0% vs 10.8%; OR=2.56) and overall morbidity (33.2% vs 17.7%; OR=2.12).
- **Supports recommendation**: Quantifies the tradeoff -- modest recurrence benefit vs significant morbidity increase
- **Full text available**: No (subscription required for Thyroid journal)
- **Data source**: Abstract

### 2E. Liang et al. 2017 -- pCND meta-analysis (6,823 patients)

- **PMID**: 28374865
- **Citation**: Liang J, Li Z, Fang F, Yu T, Li S. Is prophylactic central neck dissection necessary for cN0 differentiated thyroid cancer patients at initial treatment? A meta-analysis of the literature. *Acta Otorhinolaryngol Ital*. 2017 Feb;37(1):1-8.
- **DOI**: [10.14639/0392-100X-1195](https://doi.org/10.14639/0392-100X-1195)
- **Key finding**: 23 articles, 6,823 patients. TT+pCND had significantly higher transient RLN injury (P=0.023), transient hypocalcemia (P<0.01), and permanent hypocalcemia (P<0.01), with a trend toward lower central neck recurrence (P<0.01).
- **Supports recommendation**: Additional evidence of increased complication rates with pCND
- **Full text available**: Yes (PMC5384304)
- **Data source**: Abstract + Full text in PMC

### 2F. Wang et al. 2023 -- Most recent meta-analysis

- **PMID**: 36733809
- **Citation**: Wang Y, Xiao Y, Pan Y, Yang S, Li K, Zhao W, Hu X. The effectiveness and safety of prophylactic central neck dissection in clinically node-negative papillary thyroid carcinoma patients: a meta-analysis. *Front Endocrinol (Lausanne)*. 2023 Jan 17;13:1094012.
- **DOI**: [10.3389/fendo.2022.1094012](https://doi.org/10.3389/fendo.2022.1094012)
- **Key finding**: 15 studies. TT+PCND had lower local recurrence (OR 0.22; P=0.000) but higher permanent hypocalcemia (OR 4.24; P=0.043) and transient hypoparathyroidism (OR 2.14; P=0.001). No difference in RLN injury rates.
- **Supports recommendation**: Most recent evidence confirming recurrence-complication tradeoff; notably higher permanent hypocalcemia risk (OR 4.24)
- **Full text available**: Yes (PMC9886572)
- **Data source**: Abstract + Full text in PMC

## TOPIC 3: Surgical Complication Rate Studies

### 3A. Hauch et al. 2014 -- NIS 62,722 procedures (KEY STUDY for 7.6% vs 14.5%)

- **PMID**: 24943236
- **Citation**: Hauch A, Al-Qurayshi Z, Randolph G, Kandil E. Total thyroidectomy is associated with increased risk of complications for low- and high-volume surgeons. *Ann Surg Oncol*. 2014 Dec;21(12):3844-52.
- **DOI**: [10.1245/s10434-014-3846-8](https://doi.org/10.1245/s10434-014-3846-8)
- **Key finding**: NIS 2003-2009, 62,722 procedures. Overall complication rate: TT 20.4% vs UT 10.8% (P<0.0001). By surgeon volume: HIGH-VOLUME (>99/yr): lobectomy 7.6% vs total thyroidectomy 14.5%; INTERMEDIATE (10-98/yr): 9.9% vs 18.8%; LOW (<10/yr): 11.8% vs 24.1%. Low-volume surgeons had higher TT complications vs high-volume (OR 1.53; P=0.0083).
- **Supports recommendation**: 2025 guideline preference for lobectomy (lower complication rates) and recommendation for high-volume surgeons
- **Full text available**: No (subscription required)
- **Data source**: Abstract

### 3B. Al-Qurayshi et al. 2016 -- JAMA Otolaryngol surgeon volume outcomes

- **PMID**: 26561736
- **Citation**: Al-Qurayshi Z, Robins R, Hauch A, Randolph GW, Kandil E. Association of surgeon volume with outcomes and cost savings following thyroidectomy: a national forecast. *JAMA Otolaryngol Head Neck Surg*. 2016 Jan;142(1):32-9.
- **DOI**: [10.1001/jamaoto.2015.2503](https://doi.org/10.1001/jamaoto.2015.2503)
- **Key finding**: 77,863 patients (NIS 2003-2009). Low-volume surgeons (1-3/yr) had 15.8% complication rate vs 7.7% for high-volume (>=30/yr); OR 1.55 (95% CI 1.19-2.03; P=0.001). Mean hospital cost: high-volume $6,663 vs low-volume $10,396 (P<0.001).
- **Supports recommendation**: 2025 recommendation for referral to high-volume thyroid surgeons (>25-50 thyroidectomies/year)
- **Full text available**: No (subscription required)
- **Data source**: Abstract

## TOPIC 4: Lobectomy Eligibility for 2-4 cm Tumors

### 4A. Song et al. 2019 -- Propensity-matched 1-4 cm analysis (KEY STUDY)

- **PMID**: 30375260
- **Citation**: Song E, Han M, Oh HS, Kim WW, Jeon MJ, Lee YM, Kim TY, Chung KW, Kim WB, Shong YK, Hong SJ, Sung TY, Kim WG. Lobectomy is feasible for 1-4 cm papillary thyroid carcinomas: a 10-year propensity score matched-pair analysis on recurrence. *Thyroid*. 2019 Jan;29(1):64-70.
- **DOI**: [10.1089/thy.2018.0554](https://doi.org/10.1089/thy.2018.0554)
- **Key finding**: 2,345 patients with 1-4 cm PTC, propensity-matched (381 per group), median 9.8 years follow-up. No significant DFS difference (HR=1.35; P=0.33). Stratified: 1-2 cm (HR=1.57; P=0.228) and 2-4 cm (HR=0.93; P=0.902) both non-significant. Surgical extent was NOT an independent factor for recurrence.
- **Supports recommendation**: Lobectomy feasibility for 1-4 cm PTC; supports 2025 guideline expansion of lobectomy eligibility to <=4 cm
- **Full text available**: No (subscription required for Thyroid journal)
- **Data source**: Abstract

### 4B. Gartland & Lubitz 2018 -- Systematic review 1.0-4.0 cm

- **PMID**: 29855833
- **Citation**: Gartland RM, Lubitz CC. Impact of extent of surgery on tumor recurrence and survival for papillary thyroid cancer patients. *Ann Surg Oncol*. 2018 Sep;25(9):2520-2525.
- **DOI**: [10.1245/s10434-018-6550-2](https://doi.org/10.1245/s10434-018-6550-2)
- **Key finding**: Systematic review of 13 studies (8 national database, 1 multi-institutional, 3 large series, 1 meta-analysis). TT for PTC 1.0-4.0 cm does NOT confer clinically significant improvement in disease-specific survival vs lobectomy. 4/6 studies showed small DFS improvement with TT but clinical significance questioned.
- **Supports recommendation**: Supports lobectomy as equivalent option for 1-4 cm PTC for overall/disease-specific survival
- **Full text available**: Yes (PMC6070400)
- **Data source**: Abstract + Full text in PMC

### 4C. Zhang et al. 2020 -- Meta-analysis (TT vs LT for PTC)

- **PMID**: 32028431
- **Citation**: Zhang C, Li Y, Li J, Chen X. Total thyroidectomy versus lobectomy for papillary thyroid cancer: a systematic review and meta-analysis. *Medicine (Baltimore)*. 2020 Feb;99(6):e19073.
- **DOI**: [10.1097/MD.0000000000019073](https://doi.org/10.1097/MD.0000000000019073)
- **Key finding**: 13 studies. Overall: TT and LT had similar OS (HR=1.04; P=0.60). BUT in 2.0-4.0 cm subgroup, TT provided better OS (HR=0.88; P=0.03). LT increased recurrence risk across all sizes. CAUTION: LT associated with higher mortality in 2.0-4.0 cm tumors.
- **Supports recommendation**: Provides counterargument for lobectomy in 2-4 cm range; supports shared decision-making approach
- **Full text available**: Yes (PMC7015547)
- **Data source**: Abstract + Full text in PMC

### 4D. Suman et al. 2020 -- T1b-T2 with high-risk features

- **PMID**: 31672668
- **Citation**: Suman P, Razdan SN, Wang CE, Tulchinsky M, Ahmed L, Prinz RA, Winchester DJ. Thyroid lobectomy for T1b-T2 papillary thyroid cancer with high-risk features. *J Am Coll Surg*. 2020 Jan;230(1):136-144.
- **DOI**: [10.1016/j.jamcollsurg.2019.09.021](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jamcollsurg.2019.09.021)
- **Key finding**: NCDB, 8,083 lobectomy patients for T1b-T2 PTC. 19.2% had high-risk features (should have had TT). At 10 years, aTL 92.4% survival vs iTL 88.5% (P<0.001). iTL patients had significantly higher mortality (HR 1.97; P<0.001). Exclusion of high-risk features is critical when offering lobectomy.
- **Supports recommendation**: Supports lobectomy for T1b-T2 WITHOUT high-risk features; emphasizes importance of proper patient selection
- **Full text available**: No (subscription required)
- **Data source**: Abstract

### 4E. Bojoga et al. 2020 -- Meta-analysis of low-risk DTC (175,430 patients)

- **PMID**: 32708218
- **Citation**: Bojoga A, Koot A, Bonenkamp J, de Wilt J, IntHout J, Stalmeier P, Hermens R, Smit J, Ottevanger P, Netea-Maier R. The impact of the extent of surgery on the long-term outcomes of patients with low-risk differentiated non-medullary thyroid cancer: a systematic meta-analysis. *J Clin Med*. 2020 Jul 21;9(7):2316.
- **DOI**: [10.3390/jcm9072316](https://doi.org/10.3390/jcm9072316)
- **Key finding**: 16 studies, 175,430 patients with low-risk DTC. No significant differences between TL and TT for: recurrence (7% vs 7%; RR 1.10), OS (94.1% vs 94.4%; RR 0.99), DFS (87% vs 91%; RR 0.96), or DSS (97.2% vs 95.4%; RR 1.01).
- **Supports recommendation**: Strong evidence that lobectomy is equivalent to total thyroidectomy for low-risk DTC; supports shared decision-making
- **Full text available**: Yes (PMC7408649)
- **Data source**: Abstract + Full text in PMC

## ADDITIONAL REFERENCE

### Anderson et al. 2016 -- T1a vs T1b distinction

- **PMID**: 27266722
- **Citation**: Anderson KL, Youngwirth LM, Scheri RP, Stang MT, Roman SA, Sosa JA. T1a versus T1b differentiated thyroid cancers: do we need to make the distinction? *Thyroid*. 2016 Aug;26(8):1046-52.
- **DOI**: [10.1089/thy.2016.0073](https://doi.org/10.1089/thy.2016.0073)
- **Key finding**: 149,912 DTC patients (NCDB + SEER). After adjustment, OS (P=0.23) and DSS (P=0.93) were similar for T1a vs T1b tumors. With the 2015 ATA guidelines allowing lobectomy for low-risk tumors, treatment differences between T1a/T1b may diminish over time.
- **Supports recommendation**: Supports de-escalation of surgery for small tumors; T1 subdivision may become obsolete
- **Full text available**: Yes (PMC4976229)
- **Data source**: Abstract + Full text in PMC

## NOTES

1. The 7.6% vs 14.5% complication rates cited in the 2025 ATA guideline come from Hauch et al. (PMID 24943236), using NIS data from 62,722 procedures, specifically for HIGH-VOLUME surgeons (>99 cases/year).

2. The Chen et al. 2018 meta-analysis (PMID 29488066) is the specific study with 18,376 patients showing doubled hypocalcemia rates (OR 2.22-2.23) that drove the 2025 recommendation against routine pCND.

3. The Haigh PI reference found (PMID 12074771) is a 2002 review article on follicular thyroid carcinoma, not a large database survival analysis. Haigh may be cited in older literature but does not appear to have a major NCDB/SEER study on this specific topic.

4. For the 2-4 cm lobectomy question, evidence is MIXED: Song 2019 supports feasibility, while Rajjoub 2018 and Zhang 2020 suggest TT may be better for conventional PTC 2-4 cm. The 2025 guideline takes a nuanced approach allowing lobectomy for 2-4 cm without high-risk features with shared decision-making.

5. All abstracts retrieved via PubMed. Full text available in PMC for: Adam 2014 (PMC4532384), Adam 2015 (PMC5399499), Gartland 2018 (PMC6070400), Zhang 2020 (PMC7015547), Bojoga 2020 (PMC7408649), Wang Y 2023 (PMC9886572), Liang 2017 (PMC5384304), Anderson 2016 (PMC4976229).
